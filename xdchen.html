Brief bio

Currently, I am an exchange student at UC Berkeley. Meanwhile, I am also a graduate student affiliated to the Institute of Linguistics, Chinese Academy of Social Sciences, Beijing. Before that, I received my B.A. degree in School of Foreign Studies at Anhui University, where, advised by Jian Hu, I studied English Linguistics. In Laboratory of Phonetics and Speech Science, I am co-advised by Aijun Li and Ziyu Xiong working on prosodic acquisition of Chinese EFL learners using computational modeling. From 2018.09 to 2019.02, I was an exchange student in Department of Electronic Engineering and Information Science, USTC, where I was under the supervision of Zhen-Hua Ling and studied the automatic identification of ToBI elements in non-native speeches using neural networks.

Research Interests

Human communicates in fragmented speeches. In minutes, they can put the information encoded in the speech together and make the corresponding inference. How do we extract useful information in the noisy background using common knowledge at such speed? How do we adjust to the social networking code through verbal feedback? My research aims to answer these questions using multiple approaches including speech corpus, behavioral approaches, ERPs, neuroimaging, and machine learning.
